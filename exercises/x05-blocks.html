Edsger Dijkstra

Edsger Wybe Dijkstra (Dutch: [ˈɛtsxər ˈʋibə ˈdɛikstra] ( listen); 11 May 1930 - 6 August 2002) was a Dutch computer scientist and an early pioneer in many research areas of computing science. A theoretical physicist by training, he worked as a programmer at the Mathematisch Centrum (Amsterdam) from 1952 to 1962. He was a professor of mathematics at the Eindhoven University of Technology (1962 - 1984) and a research fellow at the Burroughs Corporation (1973 - 1984). He held the Schlumberger Centennial Chair in Computer Sciences at the University of Texas at Austin from 1984 until 1999, and retired as Professor Emeritus in 1999.

  1. Founder of Dijkstra's algorithm (single-source shortest path problem)
  2. DJP algorithm (minimum spanning tree problem)
  3. Principles of distributed computing
